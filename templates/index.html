WHAT's AROMA THERAPY?



Aromatherapy is a holistic healing treatment that utilizes natural plant extracts, known as essential oils, to promote physical, mental, and emotional well-being. These essential oils are extracted from various parts of plants, such as leaves, flowers, stems, roots, or bark, and are believed to have therapeutic properties. Aromatherapy is often used in conjunction with massage or inhalation techniques to enhance relaxation, reduce stress, alleviate pain, improve mood, and support overall health and wellness. It can be applied through methods like diffusers, topical application, or inhalation, and is commonly used in spa settings, wellness practices, and home environments.

HOW AROMA THERAPY WORKS?



Aromatherapy works by utilizing the natural scents of essential oils derived from plants. When these oils are inhaled or applied to the skin, they stimulate the olfactory system (sense of smell) and can have various physiological and psychological effects on the body and mind. The aromatic molecules of the essential oils interact with the limbic system, which is responsible for emotions, memory, and other functions. This interaction can lead to relaxation, stress reduction, mood enhancement, pain relief, and other therapeutic benefits. Additionally, when essential oils are applied to the skin, they can be absorbed into the bloodstream and exert systemic effects throughout the body. Overall, aromatherapy promotes holistic well-being by harnessing the therapeutic properties of natural plant extracts.

ACCESSIBILITY.


Aromatherapy is generally accessible through essential oils available at local stores or online. Self-administration methods, like diffusers or adding oils to bathwater, make it convenient. Affordability varies based on the cost of oils, but starting with basic oils and DIY methods can help manage expenses. It's crucial to research and purchase high-quality oils. Professional treatments may be more expensive but offer personalized care. Considerations include potential sensitivities and safety precautions.
